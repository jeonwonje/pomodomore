What is the pomodoro technique?


The pomodoro technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s.
It uses a timer, usually set to 25/30 minutes, followed by 5 minute breaks in between.
Each work-break interval is known as a pomodoro, Italian for 'tomato'.


How do I use this?


According to Wikipedia,
  1. 1. Decide on the task to be done.
  2. 2. Set the pomodoro timer (traditionally to 25 minutes).
  3. 3. Work on the task.
  4. 4. End work when the timer rings and put a checkmark on a piece of paper.
  5. 5. If you have fewer than four checkmarks, take a short break (3–5 minutes) and then return to step 2; otherwise continue to step 6.
  6. 6. After four pomodoros, take a longer break (15–30 minutes), reset your checkmark count to zero, then go to step 1.
